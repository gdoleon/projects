About the project


This project was a collaboration between Infinito, a branding consultancy firm and Mectamo, an architecture and design studio, where I worked as a product design consultant. The client was NG Restaurants a food subsidiary of Intercorp, the brief was to design a children playground for a new Popeye’s fast food restaurant. The playground had to reflect the Louisiana Kitchen and New Orleans concept behind Popeye’s.

Background   


Popeye's brand rescues the features of the original Louisiana kitchen, tapping into the regional heritage of the southern United States. Part of the brief was to work with concepts and ideas that could reflect the New Orleans heritage of the restaurant.

We took as inspiration the carnival celebrations of Mardi-Gras and in it we found interesting elements such as the music ,colours, costumes, street lights and facades that we thought were elements that could add the energy and vibration needed on a playground circuit. We used this elements to start the ideation of a dynamic experience.

The Space


The project was based on a Popeye’s restaurant, located at 895 Jose Leguia Avenue, Pueblo Libre District. The space dedicated for the project has an area of 126.00MTS2. The nature of the project asked for the design solution to be replicable to other locations with different sizes and proportions.

Users


Kids between ages of 3 - 6
At this age kids usually play in groups, encouraging interaction and sociability. Children discover role play and they often play with abstract elements, to which they give attributes according to their characteristics and context (tables, chairs, benches, sticks, etc.)

Kids between ages of 6 - 8
At this age the game is characterised by action and a sudden increase of moving activities, focusing on the development of organisational skills and psychomotor skills. Children start playing and experimenting with elements that challenge their abilities such as net walls, tunnels and other structures that enable and encourage different motor responses.

Ideation   


In the first sketches we tried to use as much available space as possible trying to create a central area surrounded by the main activities. Within the first ideas we proposed a line of slides that could take advantage of the roof height of the main windows area, beneath them a ball pit with little islands and a canopy that flew across the room. In the centre of the space a totem-like carousel with bouncing balls.

Slides & Ball Pits


After presenting the first ideas to the client and discarding some of them due to budget reasons, we began working on new proposals based on feedback. Some of the main concepts we began working on were ideas exploring a circuit through a topographic language of little hills and complimenting them with a set of slides in order to keep a dynamic flow inside the circle. Other important ideas we worked on were auditory and visual stimulation through music instruments, textures and fictional characters.

One initial idea that was interesting was the use of slides as part of the playground circuit, these slides created a tunnel-like area beneath them that added more energy to the circuit, we thought to complement this using a sort of ball pit.





Topography


We used changes in the topology of the ground to create little hills of grass of different sizes and functions that could work together to create a circuit. The idea was to achieve different activities through these different hills and create a modular system that would allow them to be configured in different ways.

We wanted to cover a certain range of activities within the different modules used, in order to offer children the possibility to use and develop their psychomotor skills, activities like climbing, jumping, crawling and balancing were necessary inside the playground, so we proceeded to refine each module and function.

Refinement   


Modules


  1. Module A is a wooden plank that works as a bridge connecting 2 C modules.

  2. Module B works as a tunnel that allows connection between inside and outside the circuit.

  3. Module C's main function is to work as a pillar for module F in order to build small bridges between modules, this allows children to develop their balance.

  4. Module D gives children the possibility to work their fine motor skills by climbing a net structure, and also playing with the space created. below.

  5. Module E can work as a small trampoline and as a concave surface for children to sit.





The playground’s modularity allows different configurations that can be suitable for a variety of spaces, modules are compatible with each other and they can make connections with a 120º angle which allows them to construct circuits that can vary in shape in order to fit different spaces. These are some examples of different configurations.

Anthropometry


Each module was designed taking in consideration both 95th (8 years old) and 5th (3 years old) percentile users. Every module has different dimensions, the biggest one having a diameter of 1.2 metres and the smaller one of 0.7 metres. They share two connection sides.

The Instruments


We also wanted to add some elements that could work as instruments that would allow children to have auditory feedback depending on the shape and material. The idea of instruments is a group of aluminium bottles, tubes and strings of different sizes, lengths and thickness.



The Band


Part of Infinito’s work was to create the graphics and characters on the walls. We incorporated the characters into a sort of mobile totem that allowed the children to rotate each body part to play with the animal and human version of each character, as we can see in the picture.
