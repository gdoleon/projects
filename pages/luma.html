Luma

2018

Luma is a point of care device and service that empowers users suffering from iron defficiency anaemia by providing them with a clear picture of their haemoglobin levels, allowing them to make decisions that can positively affect their conditions.

Research and Development

Outcome

Duration

About the project


Luma was develoed by Entia Ltd as a home point of care device and platform that empowers users suffering from iron defficiency anaemia. It provides a clear picture of haemoglobin levels that allows users to make decisions that can positively affect their health condition. The research and development was owned and conducted by Entia's internal design and engineering departments, where I took place as a Senior Product and UX Designer.

Approach   


This project was developed in parallel to co-design sessions with core users aimed at achieving a safe and user-friendly point of care device that met users' needs.



Co-design


Co-design is a process used in product development which puts the user at the heart of the development. The co-design process is iterative, meaning, each new stage builds on what was learned in the previous session.

During the study participants guided and contributed to ideas, concepts and prototypes through answering questionnaires and attending focus groups and participating on different workshops. Workshops had a more hands on approach where the user was introduced to the design process, by putting words and ideas into drawings and prototypes that helped them express their subconscious needs. This feedback informed how Entia developed the product.

Research & Development   


The different stages of the co-design sessions with users brought into play the diverse aspects of product development. Users followed a plan where topics such as; training, user interface, brand and packaging where introduced strategically as they progressed through the stages of development. They were able to engage with these topics and were encouraged to provide unbiased feedback.

Iterative Design


The user experience went through a series of iterations with the users. We used design research methods to uncover their real needs and proceed to materialise these into features that then were presented to them through different designs.

Journeys and flows were created to depict the entire user experience. We used a diverse set of tools to bring this concepts to life, these tools went from low fidelity UI flows and paper prototypes to click-through digital interfaces and high fidelity UI prototypes.
